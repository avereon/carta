Relative Coordinates

There are several ways to enter relative coordinates. This can be a simpler way to specify points rather than entering absolute coordinates. Relative coordinates always require that a prior point be available as the reference point. This is usually accomplished by entering the first point in a command. Relative coordinates are always specified by using the '@' symbol.

Examples

Orthographic Coordinates:@4,5
Polar Coordinates:@4,<30
Simplified Angle:@2<15
Angle First:@<270,4

Orthographic Coordinates

For orthographic coordinates, coordinates are specified as normal with the x and y coordinates separated by a comma.

Polar Coordinates

For polar coordinates, angles are denoted by the '<' symbol and entered in degrees. The angle coordinate may be entered before or after the radius coordinate. A further simplification is that the angle symbol may be used in place of the comma between the coordinates.
